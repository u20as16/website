Snowbording Styles

My favorite one :)

Freestyle snowboarding is any riding that includes performing tricks. In freestyle, the rider utilizes natural and man-made features such as rails, jumps, boxes, and innumerable others to perform tricks. It is a popular all-inclusive concept that distinguishes the creative aspects of snowboarding, in contrast to a style like alpine snowboarding.

Ice Skating

There are 2 types of ice skating: speed skating and figure skating. I am a figure skater, figure skating involves jumps and spins with transitions to a piece of music. There are 6 different types of jumps which can be performed with a single, double or triple rotations in the air and spins have many variations. It is scored on technical ability and performance.

A bit of history :-)

Snowboard tricks are aerials or maneuvers performed on snowboards for fun, or in competitions. Most often, these maneuvers are performed on obstacles such as jumps, halfpipes, quarterpipes, hips, handrails, funboxes, or ledges, or on the surface of the snow. Many have their origins in precursory board sports such as skateboarding and surfing.

The history of figure skating stretches back to prehistoric times. Primitive ice skates appear in the archaeological record from about 3000 BC. At the 1908 Summer Olympics in London, England, figure skating became the first winter sport to be included in the Olympics.
